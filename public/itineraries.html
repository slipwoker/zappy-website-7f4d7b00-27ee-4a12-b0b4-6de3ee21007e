Adapting the Same Route to Your Travel Style

Every itinerary flexes across three traveler profiles — solo explorers, couples, and families — so the same 7‑day route feels entirely yours. Below is how rhythm, intensity, and overnight pace shift without losing the adventure.

Solo Explorer

Route Speed Fast & flexible — early starts, spontaneous detours, and longer driving days to pack in remote corners like the Caribbean coast.
Activity Intensity High — whitewater rafting on the Pacuare, night hikes in Monteverde, and sunrise volcano summits with no schedule but your own.
Overnight Rhythm Boutique eco-lodges and social hostels — one or two nights per stop to keep momentum high.

Couples

Route Speed Balanced — scenic drives with built-in coffee-farm stops, sunset sails on Lake Arenal, and time to linger at hot springs.
Activity Intensity Moderate — guided rainforest walks, private surf lessons, and candlelit treehouse dinners replace breakneck adventure with shared wonder.
Overnight Rhythm Boutique retreats and adults-only ecolodges — two to three nights per region for deeper connection.

Families

Route Speed Gentle — shorter drive days, frequent wildlife-spotting breaks, and kid-friendly beach towns like Puerto Viejo on the Caribbean side.
Activity Intensity Low to moderate — sloth sanctuary visits, chocolate-making workshops, gentle river floats, and hands-on science at the Children's Eternal Rainforest.
Overnight Rhythm Family-run lodges with pools and connecting rooms — three nights minimum in each base to build a home-away-from-home routine.

Which Airport Fits Your Route?

Fly into San José (SJO) for itineraries weighted toward the Caribbean coast, southern Pacific, and central highlands. Choose Liberia (LIR) when your adventure focuses on Guanacaste beaches, Rincon de la Vieja, and the Nicoya Peninsula. Both gateways connect seamlessly to every 5‑, 7‑, and 10‑day route we design.
